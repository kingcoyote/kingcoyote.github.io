    Info

    The Copperlands Name Generator takes a set of words and randomly selects and combines them to form custom place names. The words are given positional weight, with the "root" being a position of 5, prefixes being less than 5 and suffixes being more than 5. The words are sorted by weight.

    After choosing a set of words, the average positional value is calcuated. A value between 5 and 7 is required to be acceptable.

    Here are some example place names, along with the positional value of each word:

    • Orange (3) Bear (5) Junction (9)
    • Strawberry (5) Creek (8)
    • Third (2) Black (5) Elm (5) Park (10)

    You can edit the word list to add or remove any words you want. Words ending with :s will be merged with the prior word. For example, the word 'burg:s' would combine with 'oak' to become 'Oakburg'. The :p value indicates the word's positional weight. Low weights like :p2 are prefixes like "Mt." or "South", while high weights like :7 are suffixes like "Village" or "Crossing".
